About me:

I am a data scientist with an application in the field of behavioral economy and traffic flow theory. As a dedicated researcher and educator, my professional journey has been marked by a relentless pursuit of knowledge and a passion for fostering academic excellence. With a solid foundation in statistical methods and data science, my research primarily delves into the intricated realms of behavioral economy (travel behavior analysis) and the application of data science in understanding traffic dynamics. I am adept at employing diverse methodologies, including discrete choice analysis, to unravel the complexities of decision-making processes. My work reflects a commitment to behavioral economics, data mining, and data fusion explorations. The analysis of the traffic flow operation is the application of my data fusion endeavors, where I emphasized lane change maneuvers on complex motorway weaving areas.

 

I collaborated on many projects such as traffic microsimulation and travel behavior analysis (in Iran), network and carbon pricing (in France), and traffic flow dynamics analysis (in Belgium, including EU-CEF project CONCORDA and the FWO project G051118N).

 

I am also experienced in curriculum development, teaching, and mentoring of MSc students.

 

Welcome to my professional space, where the pursuit of knowledge meets a genuine enthusiasm for teaching and collaborative exploration.
